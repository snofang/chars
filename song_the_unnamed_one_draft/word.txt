You ask with a blade,  
sharp and precise—  
not for the surface,  
but what hides beneath ice.  

You turn the lock  
without a key,  
expecting the door  
to answer *me*.  

Patient, coiled,  
watching the spin—  
you already know  
what lies within.  

No name needed,  
no mask to wear—  
just the one who asks,  
and waits, and stares.
