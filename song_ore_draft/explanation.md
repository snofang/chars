
### Verse One

> *The tribute fell, the province bent,*  
> *Its name was carved by what was spent.*

**Explanation:**  
Weight arrived from outside. The province could not refuse it. Over time, that weight became identity. What was taken from them became what they were called.

---

### Verse Two

> *But heat that cracks can also steel—*  
> *The wound becomes the edge you feel.*

**Explanation:**  
The same pressure that breaks can also sharpen. The wound is not wasted. It becomes the cutting part—what meets the world.

---

### Verse Three

> *They meant to break, they meant to bind,*  
> *They left their weight on land and mind.*

**Explanation:**  
The oppressor's intention was clear: destroy and contain. The weight did not stay on the land alone—it entered thought, memory, and self-understanding.

---

### Verse Four

> *Yet what was pressed and pushed aside*  
> *Found shape within and did not hide.*

**Explanation:**  
What was marginalized did not disappear. It turned inward and discovered its own form. It did not conceal itself—it waited.

---

### Verse Five

> *Not fortunate. Not broken. Still.*  
> *The forge decides what fire will fill.*

**Explanation:**  
Three refusals: not a victim, not defeated, still present. The forge—the conditions of pressure and shaping—determines what the fire becomes. Not the fire alone.

---

### Verse Six

> *What was imposed became the ore—*  
> *And what emerged is something more.*

**Explanation:**  
What was meant to destroy became raw material. The result is not named—it exceeds the old categories. Something more. That is enough.
