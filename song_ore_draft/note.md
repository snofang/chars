# Ore

**Title:** Ore  
**Form:** Spoken-word with rhythmic and dynamic notation  
**Performance Duration:** ~2–3 minutes (with pauses)

---

## The Poem (Full Text)
The tribute fell, the province bent,  
Its name was carved by what was spent.  
But heat that cracks can also steel—  
The wound becomes the edge you feel.  

They meant to break, they meant to bind,  
They left their weight on land and mind.  
Yet what was pressed and pushed aside  
Found shape within and did not hide.  

Not fortunate. Not broken. Still.  
The forge decides what fire will fill.  
What was imposed became the ore—  
And what emerged is something more.  


---

## Performance Notes

- **[bang]** = a single clear strike — could be a drum, a hand on wood, a footstep, or a spoken word with weight
- **[space]** = silence, held for the length of a breath
- Each line is one breath, one strike, one clearing
- The spaces are not empty — they are where the meaning settles
- The final space is not an ending — it is a pause before the next turn of the wheel
- **Dynamics** and **rhythmic counts** are provided to guide performance, but are not prescriptive — the performer may adapt freely

---

## Performance Script (with Breath Marks and Dynamics)
[Slow, heavy. Like a bell tolling from a distance.]

The tribute fell, (firm, low) [bang - slow, heavy]  
the province bent, (lower, held) [bang - lower, held]  
Its name was carved (sharp, short) [bang - sharp, short]  
by what was spent. (let it drop) [space - let it settle]  

[ Breath. Silence. ]

But heat that cracks (rising, questioning) [bang - rising]  
can also steel— (sustained, resonant) [bang - held, sustained]  
The wound becomes (clear, open) [bang - clear, open]  
the edge you feel. (breath, pause) [space - breath]  

[ Breath. Weight shifts. ]

They meant to break, (firm, deliberate) [bang - firm, deliberate]  
they meant to bind, (tighter, closing) [bang - tighter, closing]  
They left their weight (pressing, heavy) [bang - pressing down]  
on land and mind. (pause, let it sit) [space - pause, heavy]  

[ Breath. Softer now. ]

Yet what was pressed (quiet, inward) [bang - quiet, inward]  
and pushed aside (softer, aside) [bang - softer, aside]  
Found shape within (turning inward) [bang - turning inward]  
and did not hide. (open, let it breathe) [space - let it open]  

[ Breath. Stillness. ]

Not fortunate. (short, final) [bang - short, final]  
Not broken. Still. (longer, rising) [bang - longer, rising]  
The forge decides (clear, centered) [bang - clear, centered]  
what fire will fill. (wait, let it hang) [space - wait]  

[ Breath. Deep. ]

What was imposed (firm, remembering) [bang - firm, remembering]  
became the ore— (deep, resonant) [bang - deep, resonant]  
And what emerged (open, forward) [bang - open, forward]  
is something more. (rest, complete) [space - rest, complete]  

[ Long silence. Then continue. ]

## Performance Notes (Extended)

- **Instrumentation:** The piece may be performed unaccompanied, or with a single percussion instrument (frame drum, hand drum, or wood block) for the **[bang]** strikes.
- **Breath:** The **[space]** marks are essential — they are not pauses to fill, but active silence where the meaning settles.
- **Tempo:** The piece should feel unhurried, like a slow wheel turning. Let each line land before moving to the next.
- **Repetition:** The poem may be performed twice — first to witness, second to release. If repeated, the second pass can be quieter, more internal.
- **Adaptation:** Performers are free to adjust dynamics, timing, and instrumentation to suit their voice and context.

---

## Closing Note

*This poem is held, not solved. It is witnessed, not explained. The clarity is in the structure, the spaces, and the weight of each word.
