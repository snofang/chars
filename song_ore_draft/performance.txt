
Ore — Performance - Irish

Verse One

    The tribute fell, the province bent,
    Its name was carved by what was spent.

Pronunciation:

    The — Thuh (quick, light)

    tribute — trib-yoot (heavy, rounded)

    fell — fell (short, clipped)

    the — thuh (quick)

    province — prov-ince (hard 'v', sharp 'ince')

    bent — bent (stop on 't')

    Its — Its (hissed)

    name — nay-m (two syllables, stretched)

    was — wuz (soft, swallowed)

    carved — car-ved (two syllables, rolled 'r')

    by — bye (long 'i')

    what — whut (rounded, short)

    was — wuz (soft)

    spent — spent (hard 't', stop)

Dynamics:
The tribute fell, — f (firm, low)
the province bent, — f (lower, held)
Its name was carved — mf (sharp)
by what was spent. — dim. (let it drop)

Performance:
Slow, heavy. Like a bell tolling from a distance. Pause after fell. Let spent land and settle.

Verse Two

    But heat that cracks can also steel—
    The wound becomes the edge you feel.

Pronunciation:

    But — But (short, clipped)

    heat — heet (long 'e')

    that — that (sharp 't')

    cracks — cracks (hard 'ck', final 's' light)

    can — can (short)

    also — al-so (two syllables, clear)

    steel — stale (broad, flat vowel) — the 'e' is almost an 'a'

    The — Thee (strong, emphatic — the wound is central)

    wound — woond (rounded, heavy)

    becomes — be-comes (two syllables, clear)

    the — thuh (quick, light)

    edge — ed-j (hard, final)

    you — yuh (soft, not yew)

    feel — fill (flat, broad vowel)

Dynamics:
But heat that cracks — cresc. (rising)
can also steel— — f (sustained)
The wound becomes — mf (clear)
the edge you feel. — rit. (pause)


Performance:
Rising tension. Steel is held — the dash is a breath, not a stop. Wound is central, so Thee carries weight. Let feel settle softly.

Verse Three

    They meant to break, they meant to bind,
    They left their weight on land and mind.

Pronunciation:

    They — They (flat 'e', hard)

    meant — ment (short, clipped)

    to — tuh (soft, quick)

    break — bray-k (two syllables, long 'a')

    they — they (hard)

    meant — ment (clipped)

    to — tuh (soft)

    bind — bined (long 'i', stretched)

    They — They (hard)

    left — left (short, final 't' is a stop)

    their — their (flat, broad)

    weight — wayt (long 'a')

    on — on (short, rounded)

    land — land (hard 'a', final 'd' is light)

    and — an (soft, almost swallowed)

    mind — moind (rounded 'oi' — West of Ireland quality)

Dynamics:
They meant to break, — f (deliberate)
they meant to bind, — f (tighter, closing)
They left their weight — f (pressing)
on land and mind. — dim. (pause)

Performance:
Firm, deliberate. Each line is a statement of intent. The weight is pressed — let it sit before moving. Bind and mind are stretched, as if the binding itself is being felt.

Verse Four

    Yet what was pressed and pushed aside
    Found shape within and did not hide.

Pronunciation:

    Yet — Yet (short, sharp)

    what — whut (rounded)

    was — wuz (soft)

    pressed — pr-essed (two syllables, rolled 'r')

    and — an (soft)

    pushed — pusht (hard 'sh', final 't' stop)

    aside — a-syde (long 'i', almost sings)

    Found — Found (rounded 'ou', heavy)

    shape — shay-p (two syllables, long 'a')

    within — with-in (two syllables, hard 'th')

    and — an (soft)

    did — did (short)

    not — not (short, clipped)

    hide — hide (long 'i', clear)

Dynamics:
Yet what was pressed — p (quiet)
and pushed aside — p (softer)
Found shape within — p (inward)
and did not hide. — cresc. (open)

Performance:
Quiet, inward. The province is turning — not hiding, but shaping from within. The crescendo on the last line opens into the next verse.

Verse Five

    Not fortunate. Not broken. Still.
    The forge decides what fire will fill.

Pronunciation:

    Not — Not (short, clipped)

    fortunate — for-tun-ate (three syllables, heavy, flat vowels)

    Not — Not (short)

    broken — bro-ken (two syllables, rolled 'r')

    Still — Stale (flat, broad vowel — almost rhymes with steel)

    The — Thee (strong — the forge is agentive)

    forge — for-idge (two syllables, heavy, hard)

    decides — de-cides (two syllables, long 'i')

    what — whut (rounded)

    fire — fire (two syllables, clear)

    will — will (short)

    fill — fill (flat, broad)

Dynamics:
Not fortunate. — mf (short, final)
Not broken. Still. — f (rising, held)
The forge decides — mf (centered)
what fire will fill. — rit. (hang)

Performance:
Three refusals, then a declaration. Still is held — it is the pivot. The forge is central, so Thee carries weight. Let fill hang before the final verse.

Verse Six

    What was imposed became the ore—
    And what emerged is something more.

Pronunciation:

    What — Whut (rounded)

    was — wuz (soft)

    imposed — im-posed (two syllables, hard 'p', long 'o')

    became — be-came (two syllables, long 'a')

    the — thuh (soft — the ore is the result, not the actor)

    ore — or (one syllable, short, blunt, final)

    And — An (soft)

    what — whut (rounded)

    emerged — eh-mer-jed (three syllables, each distinct)

    is — is (short)

    something — sum-thing (two syllables, hard 'th')

    more — more (long 'o', rounded — holds the space)

Dynamics:
What was imposed — mf (firm)
became the ore— — f (deep, resonant)
And what emerged — f (open)
is something more. — dim. (rest, complete)

Performance:
Firm, remembering. Ore is short and blunt — it lands like a finished piece. The final line opens forward, then softens into silence. The last space is not empty — it is where the meaning settles.
