- **Tempo:** 60 BPM — one beat per second. Do not rush.
- **Accent:** Heavy Irish accent — vowels are broad, consonants are hard, final 't's are stops.
- **Dynamics:** The shot (Measures 16–20) should be noticeably firmer and louder than the surrounding verses.
- **Silence:** The rests are not empty — they are where the meaning settles.
- **Repetition:** The shot is repeated to isolate it from the verse. It becomes a principle, not a line.

## The Poem (Full Text)

> *The tribute fell, the province bent,*  
> *Its name was carved by what was spent.*  
> *But heat that cracks can also steel—*  
> *The wound becomes the edge you feel.*  
> *They meant to break, they meant to bind,*  
> *They left their weight on land and mind.*  
> *Yet what was pressed and pushed aside*  
> *Found shape within and did not hide.*  
> *Not fortunate. Not broken. Still.*  
> ***The forge decides what fire will fill.** *  ← shot  
> *What was imposed became the ore—*  
> *And what emerged is something more.*
