Here is a general BPM selection guide for spoken-word and poetic recitation — not for singing, but for *delivery with weight*.

---

## BPM Selection Guide — Spoken-Word Recitation

| BPM Range | Tempo Name | Feel | Best For | Example |
|:----------|:-----------|:-----|:---------|:--------|
| **80–100** | *Conversational* | Natural speech, alert, direct | Reading, quick recitation, storytelling | Telling a story to a friend |
| **70–80** | *Reflective* | Grounded, thoughtful, clear | Reciting with weight, personal delivery | Speaking a poem you know well |
| **60–70** | *Deliberate* | Heavy, intentional, unhurried | Unfolding each line, letting words land | Reciting a poem with silence between lines |
| **50–60** | *Ceremonial* | Slow, ritualistic, resonant | Formal recitation, mantra-like delivery | Speaking a poem as a rite |
| **40–50** | *Meditative* | Chant-like, each word is a stone | Deep listening, contemplative space | Speaking a poem as a prayer |
| **30–40** | *Funeral* | Extremely slow, almost suspended | Mourning, monumental weight | Speaking a poem that carries the dead |

---

## How to Choose — Decision Flow

```
Start here:
What is the poem's primary tone?

  ├── Light, playful, quick → 80–100 BPM
  ├── Narrative, storytelling → 70–80 BPM
  ├── Heavy, reflective, weighty → 60–70 BPM
  ├── Ceremonial, formal, ritual → 50–60 BPM
  ├── Meditative, contemplative → 40–50 BPM
  └── Mournful, monumental → 30–40 BPM
```

---

## How to Find Your Natural BPM

1. **Speak the poem aloud** — once, naturally, as if to someone who needs to hear it.
2. **Time yourself** — count the seconds it takes to speak the first 4 lines.
3. **Calculate**:
   - Each line has ~4 beats (measures)
   - Total beats = lines × 4
   - BPM = (total beats ÷ total seconds) × 60

**Example:**
- You speak 4 lines in 10 seconds
- Each line = 4 beats → 16 total beats
- BPM = (16 ÷ 10) × 60 = **96 BPM**

---

## Adjustment Guide

| If it feels... | Adjust by |
|:---------------|:----------|
| Too rushed, no space between lines | Drop 5–10 BPM |
| Too slow, words feel disconnected | Raise 5–10 BPM |
| Uncomfortable, forced | Drop 10–15 BPM and breathe more |
| Losing the weight of the words | Drop 10 BPM |
| Too heavy, dragging | Raise 10 BPM |

---

## The Final Rule

> **The correct BPM is the one where you stop thinking about the BPM.**

Let the poem breathe. Let the silence speak. Let the weight decide.
