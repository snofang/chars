## [1. VOWEL WEIGHT & STRESS PATTERNS]

**Key Principle:**  
Persian poetry flows on long vowels (â, u, i) and open syllables.  
Each hemistich (half-verse) has a natural stress point—usually the penultimate syllable.

---

**Verse 1 — Stress Map:**

همین که می‌فهمی، کاری از دستت برآمده.

| Syllable | Stress | Duration | Note |
|----------|--------|----------|------|
| هَـمـیـن | **Primary** | long | hold the "ـیـن" |
| کِه | unstressed | short | quick, connective |
| میـفـهـمـی | secondary | medium | stress on "ـمـی" |
| کـا | unstressed | short | light |
| رِی | secondary | medium | soft lift |
| اَز | unstressed | short | breathy |
| دَسـتـَت | secondary | medium | weight on "دَسـ" |
| بـِـرآمـدِه | **Primary** | long + fade | hold "آ"، decay on "ـدِه" |

---

**Chorus 1 — Stress Map:**

درد که می‌آید، نگاهش کن—  
اما نرو از خودت.

| Syllable | Stress | Duration | Note |
|----------|--------|----------|------|
| دَرد | **Primary** | long | heavy, grounded |
| کِه | unstressed | short | connective |
| میـآیـد | secondary | medium | stretch "آ" |
| نـِگـاهـَش | secondary | medium | stress on "گـاه" |
| کُـن | **Primary** | short + stop | clipped, firm |
| اَما | unstressed | short | soft turn |
| نَـرَو | secondary | medium | slight lift on "رَو" |
| اَز | unstressed | short | breathy |
| خـُودَت | **Primary** | long | hold "خـود" |

رفتی از خودت—چه دیدی؟

| Syllable | Stress | Duration | Note |
|----------|--------|----------|------|
| رَفـتـی | secondary | medium | rising |
| اَز | unstressed | short | quick |
| خـودَت | **Primary** | long | weight |

| دیـدی | **Primary** | long + lift | rise on "دی" |

| چِـه غَـمی | secondary → **Primary** | swell | build on "غَـم" |

## [4. SPECIAL ACCENT MARKS FOR PERFORMERS]

| Mark | Meaning | Application |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **˘** | breve (short) | on unstressed syllables like "کِه", "اَز" |
| **¯** | macron (long) | on stressed vowels like "آ", "یـن", "خـود" |
| **‵** | primary stress | the heaviest weight in the phrase |
| **′** | secondary stress | lighter but still present |
| **◌̆** | breathy | on syllables like "هـَـم" that should feel soft |
| **◌̄** | held | on final syllables that should ring |
| **>** | accent | a sharp, brief emphasis |
| **^** | swell | gradual increase in volume and weight |

## [5. PERFORMANCE NOTES FOR EACH PHRASE]

**"همین که می‌فهمی"**  
— Stress "همین" (this very thing)  
— "می‌فهمی" should feel like realization, not conclusion  
— Slight lift on "می" before settling on "فهمی"

**"کاری از دستت برآمده"**  
— Weight on "دستت" (your hand)  
— "برآمده" should feel like something rising, then settling  
— Let "آ" open wide, then let "مده" fade

**"درد را بگذار برای جایی که برآید"**  
— "درد" (pain) should feel heavy but not dramatic  
— "بگذار" (leave) should feel like releasing, not abandoning  
— "برآید" should lift slightly, as if the pain itself is moving away

**"درد که می‌آید، نگاهش کن"**  
— "می‌آید" should feel like an arrival, not a threat  
— "نگاهش کن" (look at it) should feel steady, not fearful

**"اما نرو از خودت"**  
— "اما" (but) should feel like a gentle turn  
— "نرو از خودت" (don't leave yourself) should feel like a hand on your own shoulder

**"بگذار ببیند که کسی هست"**  
— "بگذار" (let) should feel like permission  
— "کسی هست" (someone is) should feel like discovery

**"که دیدنش را تاب می‌آوری"**  
— "تاب می‌آوری" (you can bear) should feel like strength, not resignation

**"نگاه اول، خود را می‌بیند"**  
— "نگاه اول" (first look) should feel cool, distant  
— "می‌بیند" should feel like observation, not connection

**"نگاه دوم، خود را با خود می‌برد"**  
— "نگاه دوم" (second look) should feel warmer  
— "با خود می‌برد" (takes with itself) should feel like integration

**"تسلیت نمی‌گوید «حالا چه»"**  
— "تسلیت" (consolation) should feel soft, almost whispered  
— "نمی‌گوید «حالا چه»" (doesn't say "now what") should feel like relief

**"می‌گوید «با خودت هستی، همین»"**  
— "با خودت هستی" (you are with yourself) should feel like return  
— "همین" (this very thing) should feel like arrival, closure without ending

**Bridge questions:**  
— "رفتی از خودت—چه دیدی؟" should feel like a genuine question, not rhetorical  
— "ماندی در خودت—چه غمی می‌ماند؟" should feel heavier, more intense

**Outro:**  
— Should feel like the same words, but now they mean something else  
— Not "I understand, so I can act" but "I understand, so I can stay"  
— The final "همین" should feel like the center of the wheel—still, complete, at rest

## [6. FINAL DELIVERY SUMMARY]

| Element | Instruction |
|---------|-------------|
| Tone | Spoken-sung, intimate, never theatrical |
| Tempo | Andante calmo (≈72 bpm), steady, unhurried |
| Breath | Let each phrase land before the next begins |
| Dynamics | Soft overall, with controlled swells |
| Accents | On key words: همین, دستت, خودت, غم, تاب |
| Bang | Raw, unpolished, breaking through—but not violent |
| Silence | As important as the sound—let it speak |
| Ending | Fade, don't stop—let it dissolve into presence |

---

**Final Instruction for the Performer:**  
This piece is not about performing.  
It is about *being*—through sound, through silence, through return.  
The bang is not destruction. It is the moment you stop running.  
The silence after is not emptiness. It is where you finally find yourself.
